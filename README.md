Types of Newspaper Ads in Delhi

Before booking a newspaper ad in Delhi, it's important to understand the different types of ads available. Each type serves different needs, and choosing the right format is essential for maximising the effectiveness of your campaign.

Type of Ad Description 

Classified Ads Short, text-based ads that are inexpensive and ideal for job listings, real estate, services, etc. 

Display Ads: Larger, visually striking ads that include graphics, images, and detailed text. Typically placed in the main sections of the newspaper.
 Public Notice Ads are used for legal announcements, business changes, or government-related notices. 
 
Insert Ads: Separate printed materials inserted into the newspaper, ideal for special promotions or product launches. 

Combo Ads: A mix of classified and display ads, providing visibility in multiple sections.
How to Book Newspaper Ads in Delhi with Best Rates
Booking a newspaper ad in Delhi with the best rates can seem like a daunting task, but with the right approach, you can save money while ensuring your ad reaches the right audience. Here's a step-by-step process for booking an ad at the best price:
1. Understand Your Ad Budget
Determine how much you are willing to spend on the ad. Classified ads are the most cost-effective, while display ads can be more expensive. Setting a clear budget will help you choose the right ad type and placement.

2. Select the Right Newspaper
Delhi has many leading newspapers such as The Times of India, The Hindustan Times, The Hindu, and Navbharat Times. Each of these newspapers targets a different demographic, so it's important to choose the one that aligns with your target audience.

3. Leverage Online Booking Platforms
Using an online booking platform like Get Me Up Advertising can help you compare prices across various newspapers and get the best deal. These platforms often offer discounts, packages, and promotions that are not available through direct bookings.

4. Choose the Right Ad Size and Placement
Smaller ads are more affordable, but they may not have the same impact as larger ads. Determine whether a small classified ad or a full-page display ad will suit your needs. Additionally, consider the placement of your ad - ads placed on the front page or in prime sections will cost more but will also be more visible.

5. Consider Timing and Frequency
Ads placed on Sundays typically cost more due to higher readership, but you can choose weekdays for more cost-effective placements. If you plan to place multiple ads, ask about bulk discounts or package deals for long-term advertising.

6. Confirm Ad Deadline
Newspapers have strict deadlines for submitting ads, typically 2–7 days before the publication date. Make sure you submit your ad on time to avoid additional charges or missing your preferred publication date.
